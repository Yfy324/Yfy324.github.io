Research Interests

Her research focuses on intelligent fault diagnosis, early fault detection, and out-of-distribution generalization (including out-of-distribution detection and domain generalization).

Her master's degree research project is to design and implement an intelligent fault diagnosis system for water pumps under small sample conditions. She models the fault diagnosis subtask as a domain generalization problem and the fault detection subtask as an out-of-distribution detection problem, taking into account the lack of high-quality training data and the unavailability of testing data at the training stage. She believes that proposing algorithms with better generalization performance and more robustness can significantly facilitate the application of machine learning in industrial scenarios.

Her current research interests lie in out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization and its applications. OOD generalization can improve the utilization of training data, which is related to the basic research of machine learning. With OOD generalization algorithms, researchers can relax the restrictions on the number of training samples, the quality of labels, the independent and identical distribution of training and test data, and the accessibility of testing data at the training stage. On the other hand, research in this field makes it possible to use machine intelligence to promote economic development and improve the standard of living of people because this field has close connections to practical tasks, e.g., fault detection and diagnosis, medical diagnosis, fraud detection in finance, etc. She regards finding invariant causal mechanisms as a promising solution to OOD generalization.
